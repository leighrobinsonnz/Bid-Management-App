Bid Management Process Transformation

This programme introduces a structured, lifecycle-aligned approach to how we run bids.

Why this matters

  • ⚠strong work already happens across teams, but not always in a consistent flow
  • ⚠this can create unnecessary pressure, rework, and variability

The goal is simple

  • ✓ensure the right inputs happen at the right time
  • ✓establish clear ownership at each stage
  • ✓enable smoother end-to-end bid execution

Lifecycle-Aligned Bid Execution Model

The bid execution model aligns each stage of the bid lifecycle to Salesforce opportunity stages, creating clear handoffs, ownership and approval gates.

1
Opportunity Identification
Purpose: Identify and capture opportunity Owner: Sales Executive
2
Bid Qualification
Purpose: Determine bid viability Owner: Sales + Bid Manager
3
Bid Planning
Purpose: Structure approach Owner: Bid Manager
4
Proposal Development
Purpose: Create response Owner: Bid Team
5
Commercial Approval
Purpose: Validate pricing & terms Owner: Commercial
6
Submission
Purpose: Submit proposal Owner: Bid Manager
7
Outcome & Learning
Purpose: Capture insights Owner: Bid + Sales

1. Opportunity Identification

Sales stage: Target & Hook

Identify and capture an opportunity worth progressing.

Key actions

  • Confirm the opportunity is real and worth early attention
  • Capture initial customer context and likely bid shape
  • Identify early stakeholders and timing signals

Gate criteria

  • Opportunity is logged
  • Basic customer context is known
  • There is enough signal to move into qualification

2. Bid Qualification

Sales stage: Lead Qualification

Determine whether the bid is worth pursuing.

Key actions

  • Confirm strategic fit and customer alignment
  • Assess win probability and competitive position
  • Identify key risks, dependencies, and constraints
  • Engage key stakeholders early (Sales, Presales, Delivery)

Gate criteria

  • Clear problem and customer need is defined
  • Initial solution direction is understood
  • Key stakeholders are identified and engaged
  • Decision made to proceed (or stop)

3. Bid Planning

Sales stage: Opportunity Validation

Structure the bid approach, ownership, and plan before development begins.

Key actions

  • Confirm bid scope, submission requirements, and timeline
  • Assign owners across Sales, Bid, Presales, Product, and Delivery
  • Identify pricing, delivery, and approval dependencies early
  • Define the response structure, win themes, and plan of attack

Gate criteria

  • Bid plan is agreed and owners are clear
  • Timeline is realistic and key milestones are set
  • Critical dependencies and risks are visible
  • The bid is ready to move into development

4. Proposal Development

Sales stage: Solution Proposal

Create a complete, compelling, and compliant proposal.

Key actions

  • Develop the proposal response aligned to win themes
  • Collaborate across Sales, Presales, Product, and Delivery
  • Build pricing, commercials, and delivery approach
  • Review content for quality, consistency, and compliance

Gate criteria

  • All response sections are complete and aligned
  • Pricing and delivery approach are validated
  • Internal reviews are completed (Red Team / DFA where required)
  • Proposal is ready for commercial approval

5. Commercial Approval

Sales stage: Solution Proposal

Confirm the commercial position before final submission.

Key actions

  • Validate pricing, margin, and commercial assumptions
  • Confirm delivery effort, timeline, and implementation inputs
  • Resolve outstanding risks, approvals, and exceptions
  • Prepare the bid for final internal sign-off

Gate criteria

  • Pricing and commercials are approved
  • Delivery inputs are confirmed and understood
  • Required approvers have reviewed the bid
  • The proposal is cleared to move to submission

6. Submission

Sales stage: Solution Proposal

Submit a complete, approved, and compliant proposal.

Key actions

  • Complete final checks for accuracy, formatting, and compliance
  • Ensure all approvals are documented and in place
  • Submit the proposal in line with customer requirements
  • Confirm submission and communicate next steps internally

Gate criteria

  • Proposal is submitted on time and in the correct format
  • All required approvals are captured and recorded
  • No outstanding risks or gaps remain
  • Handover to evaluation or negotiation phase is clear

7. Outcome & Learning

Sales stage: Contracting & Close

Capture outcomes, insights, and lessons to improve future bids.

Key actions

  • Capture bid outcome (win, loss, or no decision)
  • Conduct win/loss review with key stakeholders
  • Identify what worked well and what could improve
  • Share insights and update assets, templates, or approach

Gate criteria

  • Outcome is recorded and communicated
  • Key learnings are captured and shared
  • Improvements are identified for future bids
  • Relevant updates are fed back into tools and process

How We Measure Impact

Success will be measured by both efficiency improvements and quality outcomes—ensuring that better structure and guidance translates into real adoption, faster execution and improved win rates.

What will change in behaviour

More structured progression

Deals move through defined stages with clear qualification and handoffs.
→ Drives: Faster proposal preparation and reduced approval cycle time

Higher asset usage

Sellers use approved templates and guidance instead of recreating content.
→ Drives: Higher asset reuse rate and improved win rate

Earlier issue identification

Risks and pricing issues surface earlier in the lifecycle.
→ Drives: Faster bid turnaround time and fewer late-stage delays

Less rework

Late-stage changes reduce due to clearer expectations upfront.
→ Drives: Shorter cycle times and higher on-time submission rates

Bid Management Process Transformation

This programme introduces a structured, lifecycle-aligned approach to how we run bids.

Why this matters

  • ⚠strong work already happens across teams, but not always in a consistent flow
  • ⚠this can create unnecessary pressure, rework, and variability

The goal is simple

  • ✓ensure the right inputs happen at the right time
  • ✓establish clear ownership at each stage
  • ✓enable smoother end-to-end bid execution

Who does what?

Clear ownership reduces duplication, improves handoffs, and keeps bids moving.

Each stage has one owner, with clear supporting roles.

Sales
Owns opportunity + strategy
Provides inputs (win themes, key messages)
Product Marketing Manager
Shapes positioning
Writes executive summary
Provides proof points + CVP alignment
Bid Manager
Runs process + timing
Ensures inputs are captured early
Presales
Own solution + costing inputs

Supporting roles & approvals

These roles are engaged at key points to shape cost, feasibility, and approval.

Delivery
Defines deployment scope, effort, and timeline
Provides implementation cost inputs
Confirms delivery feasibility and risks
Product
Validates solution alignment and capability
Supports non-standard or new requirements
Provides input on roadmap and constraints
Key Approvers
Approve pricing, risk, and commercial position
Provide go / no-go decisions at key stages
Ensure alignment to business and financial targets

What this means in practice

  • ✓Sales leads the opportunity, not the document
  • ✓Bid Manager owns the process, not the strategy
  • ✓Inputs are gathered early, not chased late

Lifecycle-Aligned Bid Execution Model

The bid execution model aligns each stage of the bid lifecycle to Salesforce opportunity stages, creating clear handoffs, ownership and approval gates.

1
Opportunity Identification
Purpose: Identify and capture opportunity Owner: Sales Executive
2
Bid Qualification
Purpose: Determine bid viability Owner: Sales + Bid Manager
3
Bid Planning
Purpose: Structure approach Owner: Bid Manager
4
Proposal Development
Purpose: Create response Owner: Bid Team
5
Commercial Approval
Purpose: Validate pricing & terms Owner: Commercial
6
Submission
Purpose: Submit proposal Owner: Bid Manager
7
Outcome & Learning
Purpose: Capture insights Owner: Bid + Sales

1. Opportunity Identification

Identify and capture an opportunity worth progressing.

Key actions

  • Confirm the opportunity is real and worth early attention
  • Capture initial customer context and likely bid shape
  • Identify early stakeholders and timing signals

Templates & tools

Open Opportunity in Salesforce Run Initial Qualification Create Stakeholder Map

Gate criteria

  • Opportunity is logged
  • Basic customer context is known
  • There is enough signal to move into qualification

2. Bid Qualification

Determine whether the bid should proceed.

Key actions

  • Run a structured bid / no-bid assessment
  • Check strategic fit, competitiveness and resourcing
  • Confirm whether Datacom should invest in the bid

Templates & tools

  • Bid qualification checklist
  • No-bid / proceed decision support
  • Salesforce qualification fields

Gate criteria

  • Decision to proceed is clear
  • Strategic fit is confirmed
  • Owner and next step are agreed

3. Bid Planning

Set the bid up properly before development begins.

Key actions

  • Confirm scope, timeline and bid strategy
  • Assign contributors and agree responsibilities
  • Set review points and delivery plan

Templates & tools

  • Bid plan template
  • RACI / owner mapping
  • Timeline or milestone tracker

Gate criteria

  • Bid plan is agreed
  • Owners are clear
  • Delivery approach is realistic

4. Proposal Development

Develop the response and bring the proposal together.

Key actions

  • Draft and refine response content
  • Coordinate SMEs, reviews and inputs
  • Strengthen clarity, quality and customer relevance

Templates & tools

  • Response templates
  • Bid library assets
  • Review checklist

Gate criteria

  • Response is complete
  • Core quality checks are done
  • Proposal is ready for commercial review

5. Commercial Approval

Validate pricing, terms and commercial position.

Key actions

  • Review pricing, margin and commercials
  • Check assumptions and approval thresholds
  • Resolve any issues before submission

Templates & tools

  • Pricing workbook
  • Approval matrix
  • Commercial review checklist

Gate criteria

  • Pricing is approved
  • Commercial risks are understood
  • Submission can proceed

6. Submission

Complete final checks and submit the proposal.

Key actions

  • Run final review and sign-off
  • Check submission requirements and deadlines
  • Submit and confirm receipt where needed

Templates & tools

  • Submission checklist
  • Final review checklist
  • Portal / submission guidance

Gate criteria

  • All approvals are complete
  • Submission is compliant
  • Final package is issued on time

7. Outcome & Learning

Capture learning and improve the next bid.

Key actions

  • Run debrief and capture feedback
  • Document lessons learned
  • Feed insights back into assets and process

Templates & tools

  • Win / loss review template
  • Lessons learned log
  • Improvement tracker

Gate criteria

  • Outcome is recorded
  • Insights are captured
  • Useful improvements are fed back into the system

Key Design Principles

▼

Lifecycle Alignment

Every bid stage maps directly to Salesforce opportunity stages for consistency.

Clear Ownership

Each stage has defined owners and clear handoff points.

Right-Sized Gates

Approvals are positioned to support quality without creating delays.

Success will be measured by both efficiency improvements and quality outcomes—ensuring that better structure and guidance translates into real adoption, faster execution and improved win rates.

More structured progression

→ Drives: Faster proposal preparation and reduced approval cycle time

Higher asset usage

→ Drives: Higher asset reuse rate and improved win rate
